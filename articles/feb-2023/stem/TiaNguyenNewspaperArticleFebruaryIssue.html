What about sleep?

Written by: Tia N. and Taylor T.

With school on top of activities during the evenings it becomes difficult for students to get the daily amount of sleep they need. Finishing homework along with after school activities and extracurricular sports has teenagers pushing back their bedtime. The average middle school student should be receiving 8-10 hours of sleep per night. If they do not receive the correct amount of sleep it leaves a higher probability for mental health and physical health issues. Sleep helps students have a better attention span and feel more awake during school. It helps rebuild muscles, remove toxins from the brain, and maintains a healthy body.  Kids need to receive enough sleep while dealing with the challenges of life.

How much sleep should you be receiving?

        Sleeping is a part of the daily routine for every student. Waking up feeling tired may be a sign you are not sleeping enough. The CDC (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention) recommends kids from 6-12 years old should receive 9-12 hours of sleep per night. Kids from the ages of 13-18 should be getting 8-10 hours. If a student's schedule is too busy they should try to cut down on afterschool activities. Their parents should be aware and help provide a better sleep schedule. Studies from pediatrician Michael Crocetti have been published by John Hopkins Medicine, “Teens need 9 to 9 ½ hours of sleep per night-that’s an hour or so more than they needed at age 10.”  That may seem like a long time for a busy schedule, but the benefits of sleeping enough are immense.

Benefits of sleep

Students need enough sleep to do well in school. Getting the correct amount of rest leads to many helpful benefits including better productivity and clearer thought processes. Getting enough sleep leads to being more productive in school. Being able to clearly and quickly problem solve is very important in school. The sleep foundation claims, “Updating these connections in the brain during sleep helps individuals learn new information and form memories. Quality sleep leads to improved concentration and better problem-solving skills.” Sleep helps the brain better process information and increases productivity which is necessary for test taking and projects.

What happens when you don’t sleep enough:

Sleep is important and without sleep many health problems will start to affect your daily life. Memory issues, lack of concentration, and mood swings, are issues that come without sleeping enough. Without enough rest, your short term and long term memory are affected as your brain is not able to make connections that help you process and remember new information. Your mood could change making you quick-tempered, emotional, and moody which could lead up to anxiety or depression. According to NIH (National Institute of Health), they address that, “Together with impairments of attention, alertness and memory, sleep loss has consistently been associated with subjective reports of irritability and emotional volatility (Horne 1985)”.

Not only does sleep deprivation affects your mental health but also your physical health. While sleeping, your immune systems are producing protective, infection-fighting bacteria like antibodies to fight off invaders. Lack of sleep prevents your immune system from doing this and this causes your body to not be able to fend off invaders, taking a longer time for you to recover from an illness. During sleep, your body gets to work as it repairs itself and rebuilds muscles that have been worn down throughout the day while it also removes toxins from the brain. The NIC (National Institute of Health) says that, “sleep deprivation can affect your body’s ability to regulate essential functions like appetite control, your immune system, metabolism, and body weight”. Sleep helps you maintain a healthy body and that’s what a growing body needs.

                                        Conclusion

Sleep has an important role for your mental and physical health. Lack of sleep will cause many health complications and will affect your daily life. With a still growing body, teenagers need to get a daily amount of sleep that will help them grow into a healthy body. Take it upon yourself to watch your bedtime and get enough rest, so you can feel awake and healthy the next day. Your health depends on your sleep, so go to bed on time.
