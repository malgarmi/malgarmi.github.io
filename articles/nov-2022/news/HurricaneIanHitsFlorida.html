On September 23rd, a tropical depression formed near southeast Jamaica. In under 24 hours, it formed into a category 3 hurricane. This hurricane has destroyed cities, landmarks, and homes and it took the lives of many people.  According to the washingtonpost.com, Hurricane Ian has made it to the list of the top 5 most dangerous hurricanes to have hit the United States. Now let’s explore more about Hurricane Ian!

The Hurricane

Before you learn what Hurricane Ian is, you may be wondering what hurricanes are as a whole. Hurricanes are violent windstorms that are formed when the warm, moist air over water begins to rise. This is why hurricanes typically occur in the Atlantic Basin, which according to the National Ocean Service is the Atlantic Ocean, Caribbean Sea, Gulf of Mexico, and parts of the eastern and central Pacific Ocean. Florida is near the Atlantic, so it is especially prone to hurricanes. Hurricanes are also divided into categories using the Saffir Simpson Hurricane Wind Scale, a scale from 1-5 (1 being the least dangerous and 5 being the most) that predicts the potential damage and impact of a hurricane, as said by the National Weather Service.

Hurricane Ian originated as a tropical wave from the coast of West Africa. Soon after on September 21st, it would move towards the Caribbean Sea and into Trinidad, Tobago, the ABC Islands, and the northern coast of South America. Then on the 23rd, it became a tropical storm while it was located in southeast Jamaica. And in under 24 hours, Tropical Storm Ian became a high-end Category 3 hurricane, which we now call Hurricane Ian.

The first area it struck was Cuba, causing major flooding, which resulted in a nationwide power outage. After it went over Cuba, it approached the southeastern Gulf of Mexico, where its power increased to a Category 4. From here, Hurricane Ian neared southwest Florida, which according to the National Environmental Satellite Data and Information Service had experienced a similar hurricane in Hurricane Michael 4 years prior.

On September 29th, Hurricane Ian would go down to a tropical storm after it went inland to Florida. But as soon as it crossed the Atlantic, it again grew to a hurricane, this time a Category 1. It made its second landfall over South Carolina, but it would eventually transition to a post-tropical cyclone. This cyclone would dissipate in Virginia on October 1st, bringing an end to the storm. However, its effects have caused immense pain to its victims, physically, mentally, and economically.

The Destruction and The Impact

Hurricane Ian caused a massive amount of damage to Florida physically and financially. Hurricane Ian wiped out many jobs across southwest Florida leaving Collier County and many other cities struggling to get back on their feet. Many residential areas in Collier have been desecrated causing millions of dollars worth of destruction. Naples Daily News says that, “The damage includes the cities Naples and Marco Island $2.1 billion for residential buildings, and $147 million dollars for commercial buildings.” This much money adding on to additional damage totals to about 2.2 billion! Separately, Collier faced $948 million, and Everglades City has $7.1 million at its feet. Marco Island has to find $256 million to restore everything. Since then, people have been making progress to return life back to normal.

Florida has suffered a lot of physical damage as Hurricane Ian caused lots of damage to properties, especially in the residential areas of Florida. Cynthia King, an elderly woman, was driving to a friend's house until the hurricane hit and she was told to evacuate. She can't go back to her home because the house she lived in is now uninhabitable because of the Hurricane. “There are ups and downs to living in Florida. I have been sitting here a long time and I just want someone who can take me home,” stated King. Elderly people with special medical conditions had it much harder. Hurricanes caused destruction in many areas. For example, Central Florida and Orlando are almost completely flooded and submerged in water. People now have to swim or buy a boat in order to get across or to get supplies. In addition, it had also flooded Barrier island. Even after all of this destruction the communities came together and helped one another to restore everything. It has been a month since the hurricane hit. Right now they are busy rebuilding Fort Myers Beach and Pine Island. Slowly but surely, Florida is getting back on track. However, the road to recovery will take months, even years.

The Deaths

More than a hundred people died in Hurricane Ian. Around 60 of them drowned in the hurricane. Usually, only about 30-40 people die in hurricanes every year according to “Fact-Statistics-Hurricanes.”. The death toll varied over the past three weeks since the incident. At first it was 77, but then this week it raised higher than 100. People believe the exact number of deaths was 119. This is a shockingly high number of deaths, but when compared to Hurricane Katrina back in 2005, this isn’t that big of a number. Hurricane Katrina killed more than 1,800 people since it was ranked as a category 5 hurricane. Since Hurricane Ian was just ranked as a hurricane category 3, the death toll rose to 119.

Hurricane Ian has caused millions of people harm, and has affected many areas drastically, especially Florida. Hurricane Ian’s merciless winds destroyed many homes and ended many lives. Funerals are being held and people are still mourning for their loved ones.
