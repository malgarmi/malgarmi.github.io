Top 5

Horror Movies of 2022

                                                                  By: Gisele, Liliana, Ynez

Do shivers go down your spine watching horror movies? The year is coming to an end and we will be discussing the horror movies in theaters that made it to the top 5 this year. The top 5 horror movies that were streaming in theaters were Scream(5), Smile, Hocus Pocus 2, Morbius, and The Invitation. Although these movies are horror and Rated-R, some of these movies are family friendly.

   WARNING: SOME OF THESE MOVIES ARE RATED-R AND SHOULD BE SUPERVISED BY A PARENT/GUARDIAN IF THE CHILD IS UNDER THE AGE OF 18.

5) The Invitation

The Invitation was released August 26th, 2022 and is PG-13. This movie consists of vampires and dark secrets. A girl named Evie loses her mother to cancer and her father passed away when she was 14. Evie takes a DNA test because she’s curious to learn more about her family history. To her surprise, she discovers that  she has a long distant cousin she never knew about. She then gets invited to a wedding by her newly founded family in the English countryside. She is at first seduced by the host of the wedding but then realizes she stepped into a  

nightmare. She learns about dark twisted secrets in her family's history and the intentions behind her sinful generosity. People seem to enjoy The Invitation because it’s about vampires and people who enjoy mystery.

4) “Smile”

Smile was released September 30th, 2022 and is a Rated-R movie. Smile is psychological  horror film based on a therapist witnessing the suicide of a patient and goes through disturbing experiences that make her believe she’s supernatural. Everyone believes the therapist is a psychopath and nobody believes what she sees. She sounds so crazy that even her  family does not believe her and thinks she's hallucinating. I didn’t really like how it wasn’t really that scary, and how I was just waiting for the jumpscares. It really was a good movie but it could have been better.

 

3) Hocus Pocus 2

Hocus Pocus 2 came out September 30th on Disney Plus and is rated PG. Hocus Pocus 1 came out in 1993 . There have been plays about Hocus Pocus, and people dressing up as “Sanderson Sisters” for Halloween. The Sanderson Sisters are Winifred, Sarah, and Mary. The main character, Becca, receives a black candle on her birthday and brings the sisters back. This movie is a great movie for kids and a lot of people enjoyed it because the Sanderlon Sisters came back! It was a good movie, but it could have been a little bit better. I didn’t really like how the sisters came back in the forest, and I also didn’t really like how the cat, Thackery, wasn’t in the movie. But other than that I really liked it.

2) Morbius

Morbius was released as of Apr 1, 2022 and is PG-13.  Morbius is a Marvel/Horror movie that was based on a comic book. The movie is about a scientist who is diagnosed with a rare blood disorder and attempts to cure himself by fusing his DNA with vampire bat DNA. Morbius was given the side effects of super speed, super strength,  echolocation abilities, and an appetite for blood. Overall, this movie was good, and the comic book was just as good! People seemed to enjoy the vampire, Morbius, and the side affects!

 1) Scream

Scream has been released as of January 14th, 2022 and is a Rated-R movie. The movie is about masked murders over the past 25 years in the town of Woodsboro. InScream 5, there were two murders and the ending left people shocked because the movie had a huge twist. The movie was originally inspired by Dainel Roling - a man who murdered five students in Florida during the 1990s. The first Scream movie came out on December 20th, 1996 and there have been 5 sequels since then. Overall, a lot of people enjoyed this movie because of how the movie was filmed and how Scream 5 finally came out after 11 years! Also, In the future there will possibly be a Scream 6.

These movies we’ve listed were some of the horror movies that people were pleased with this year. In conclusion, these movies were excellent and hopefully next year we get more enjoyable movies to watch!
