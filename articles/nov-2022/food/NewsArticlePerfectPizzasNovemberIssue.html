Perfect Pizzas

Written by: Emily Cho, Jade Meyer and Ian Choi
Edited by: Jade Meyer, Emily Cho
Digital Media by: Jade Meyer 

        Today, pizza is the world's most popular food. Anywhere you are there is bound to be a pizza place down the block or a quick drive away. If there isn't, there's always delivery. According to History Today, 94% of Americans have said they have had pizza in the past week and 74% say they can have it three meals a day. Three billion pizzas are sold yearly in the United States, so the average U.S. citizen would have about 46 pizza slices per year. Pizza is loved everywhere, especially here in America. Fremont doesn’t have the finest pizza places in the world so we will be reviewing several local pizzerias in your area so you don’t have to. As we can't taste every pizza place in Fremont, we will visit and try the most well-known ones. We will rate Pieology, Little Caesars, Round Table, Dominos, and Mountain Mike’s pizzas. To ensure consistency, their small pepperoni pizzas will be reviewed. However, some pizza places don’t have the option to customize the size. We will critique the pizzas’ crust, cheese, pepperoni, and texture.

        Number 5: Pieology

Down Paseo Padre and into the Central District of Downtown Fremont, you will find Pieology directly next to an empty Sweet Tomatoes building. We ordered a small pepperoni pizza for $10.99 on the phone and it was ready within twenty minutes. The pizza had a layer of liquid grease on the cheese. It pooled on the cheese as if it were a layer of ice over a lake. The crust was extremely thin and the edges were very crispy. However, the part of the crust with any toppings on it was limp because of the grease. If you were to hold it at the end  of the crust, it would sag down and have the cheese slowly sludge off. The crust wasn’t doughy, but it definitely wasn’t solid. The crust tasted like white bread but had a wet cardboard-like texture. There were exactly six pieces of pepperoni on the whole pizza, which was not what they advertised. In various advertisements, you can see it almost completely covered in pepperoni, like the one below.

       

        The advertised pepperoni pizza at Pieology

The cheese was oddly colored. It was almost completely white, but still yellow, like the color of a banana. It was too salty, as if the worker added several spoonfuls of salt onto it. Other than the sauce, the only ingredient with decent flavor was the peperoni. The pepperoni was okay. It was a bit too salty and barely had a meat taste to it. It tasted like a brown paper bag with lumps of salt and tomato sauce on it. The tomato sauce helped the cheese taste less salty and made the pepperoni’s flavor pop. We were disappointed and hoped for more. The overall rating of this pizza is 3.5/10.

 Number 4: Little Caesars


Little Caesars is located on Thornton Avenue, directly across from Thornton Junior High. You can always rely on them when you need a quick lunch pizza. Little Caesars didn’t have a small pizza option, so we just bought the regular. The bottom of the pizza was perfect and didn’t get in your way while eating your pizza. It wasn’t soggy and had a pleasant texture. When held in the air, it sagged around 45 degrees downward, a generally appropriate amount. Even with the cheese being thick, it didn’t stretch and was rather stringy, but it still tasted like cheese. The pepperoni is overly salty but is crunchy at the ends. The sauce is chunkier and you can taste the herbs in it. The sauce made the pepperoni taste better and lifted the pizza’s overall flavor. The sauce complimented each of the flavors and textures. In summary, it received a 6/10.

Number 3: Round Table 

You will find a parking lot from Fremont Boulevard and onto Chapel Way, and you will see a Round Table directly across it. The pizza was ready in fifteen minutes. The pepperoni on the pizza was the only part with grease. The pepperoni was so crispy that it curved upwards into a bowl shape. The edges of the pepperoni were lightly brown and crunchy. A key component of the pizza was the overall texture and the fact that the saltiness didn't overpower the mozzarella flavor. The cheese was thick but still stretchy and yeasty. The cheese had a consistent flavor that was different from the other pizzas because of how it tasted and reminded everyone of bread. But, the crust was the pizza’s downfall. The crust was thinly layered. It looked like a stack of flimsy pieces of tagboard thrown on top of each other and tasted like it, too. We found the crust to be thick and difficult to bite. We had to use both hands to rip off a piece of the crust to eat. It was ironic how the cheese tasted more like bread than the actual bread base. If you were to hold the pizza at the crust, it would stay at a one-hundred-eighty-degree angle. Due to the scarce ratio of sauce on the pizza compared to the amount of other ingredients, we could not taste the tomato sauce. Overall, the pizza is a 6.5/10,

Number 2: Domino’s


        On Fremont Boulevard almost right across from Washington High School, is a Domino’s pizzeria. They serve many entrees and treats, but pizza is their main attraction. We ordered the small pepperoni pizza on the phone for $12.99 and it was ready within twenty minutes. Despite looking very airy and puffy, the crust crunched and was on the harder side when we bit into it. The crust didn’t have any give if you held it by the end of the crust. It stayed completely flat. There was barely any grease on the pizza. The cheese was milky white in color and didn’t necessarily taste like cheese. Still a dairy flavor to it but not cheese. There was a medium amount of pepperoni on the pizza, and it wasn’t too bad. It had a slight chew and overall the texture was not the most pleasant. It felt like it was frozen, thawed, frozen and thawed again. Even with the texture being very off, it tasted very good. The sauce had a rich flavor and you could taste the tomato. The sauce definitely led this pizza to get a 7/10 rating.

Number 1: Mountain Mikes


        On Thornton Avenue by Dollar Tree, you’ll find Mountain Mike’s pizzeria. We ordered another small pizza over the phone for $12.99 and it was ready in twenty-five minutes.The pizza had little to no grease on it, enhancing the flavor and making the overall texture more defined. The crust on the outside was crunchy and the inside was light and airy. The pepperoni did not muffle the cheese’s flavor. The cheese was savory, not too chewy, and still stretched nicely. The texture was great. You can taste the tomato in the sauce and the consistency was smooth. The ingredients complimented each other, something none of the other pizza places managed to do. It tasted spicy, but had a strange aftertaste. Once you swallow the pepperoni, it would feel like swallowing an eraser. We felt the subtle taste of rubber at the back of our throats. Even with the weird pepperoni the pizza, as a whole, this pizza got a rating of 8/10.

Concluding Thoughts

We've rated 5 pizza places in your area so you don’t have to. First, the highest rated pizza is Mountain Mike's with 8/10. Then Domino's with 7/10 followed by Round Table with 6.5/10. Little Caesars had a 6/10, and last, Pieology with 3.5/10. We may have judged these pizzas harshly but in general all of these pizzas are good and are a great option for a quick meal for the day.
