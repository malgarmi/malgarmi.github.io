How to Get a Higher IQ

By: Evan, Marleau, Goku, and Ricky

        

        

Introduction

Do you struggle in school to have A's and B's? This might be because you have a low IQ, but do not worry if you follow these three steps; your C's, D's, and F's will turn into A's and B's! Test-iq.org states that the average IQ of a 13-year-old is between 40-65, and the average of a 14-year-old is 60-85. These tips will not only help your IQ, but they will also help you stay more focused in school.

Sleep!

You need more sleep! Cdc.gov states that average middle school kids need 8-10 hours of sleep. However, 59% of middle school students fail to meet the required amount! Sleep causes people to have better concentration. It also helps us analyze our daily life much more efficiently. Sleep also makes us less grumpy, which causes us to be more positive. James Clear authorizes the #1 New York Times bestseller Atomic Habits. Atomic Habits is a book about good and bad habits. The book sold over 10 million copies and was copied into 50 different languages! James Clear's article says that a positive mindset opens your mind to more options, causing it to learn more things quickly. More sleep equals a better mood, which equals better grades.  

        Read!

Another way to become more innovative is to read. Healthline.com states that reading any book will help these functions: attention, predicting, working memory, long-term storage memory, abstract reasoning, comprehension, and visual processing of letters. Reading will also help you have a better imagination and vocabulary. Researchers from Yale School of Public Health found that reading novels increases livability by up to two years. Set some time to read a book for about 15-30 minutes every day, so you can keep your brain active.

Tutoring!

The last way to improve your IQ is by attending after-school classes. After-school classes can help you with the topics you struggle with. Though most after-school classes cost money, there are some free classes. Cradle Education is a website that offers you free classes. Free math, science, history, physics, and chemistry classes are available. The Alameda county library also offers free classes. All you need to do is to sign up on the Alameda County library website. These classes will dramatically improve your IQ and improve your grades.

Conclusion

If you follow these steps, you might be the next Albert Einstein! Sleeping 8-10 hours a night will make you more optimistic and get better grades. If you also read 15-30 minutes a day, you will grow your vocabulary and IQ. Our last tip is to get after-school classes, so you get any extra help you need on your work. These steps will help you increase your IQ.
