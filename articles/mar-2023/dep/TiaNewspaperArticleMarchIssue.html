Depression Among Teens: What you should know

                                By:  Taylor T. , Tia N. , Shreyas B. , and Amaya T.

                                                                                                                         

                                                 

 

             

Depression is a dangerous mental health issue that has greatly increased in teenagers and adolescents and their school and daily life could be majorly affected with just depression.  Lack of motivation, and behavioral issues are problems that can come in and affect the things you do. In the past few years the amount of adolescents experiencing depression has increased. A main cause of stress is school. Practicing self care can make you feel better. Depression is a huge issue which can not be fixed immediately, but taking time for yourself to decompress and relax, is a huge step to feeling better.

What is Depression?

Depression is known to be a mental disorder, it is very common in people. According to the  World Health Organization, it is estimated that 5% of adults suffer from this disorder. It can also disturb one’s sleep and appetite. Depression has many causes such as stressful events, alcohol and drugs, loneliness, illness, and etc. The effects of depression can be long-lasting or recurring and can dramatically affect the way a person functions and a satisfying life. Fortunately, depression has a cure. MDE, a term sometimes used meaning Major Depressive Episode. A Major Depressive Episode is a period of time where someone experiences some symptoms of depression. Some signs a person is facing depression are, (According to Depression (major depressive disorder) - Symptoms and causes - Mayo Clinic) are feelings of sadness, emptiness, hopelessness, angry outbursts and more.

  The Rise of Depression

In the past few years children’s mental health issues have increased.  Beginning in 2020 there was a 24% increase in mental health services for children ages 5 to 11. For 12 to 17 year old adolescence they experienced a 31% increase in required mental health services. In 2022, about 15% of children and teens ages 12-18 have faced depressive episodes in the past years. A depressive episode is a time period where one experiences symptoms of depression such as tiredness and changes in appetite. According to Cleveland Clinic depression is caused in children and teens due to drugs and alcohol, environment (such as family issues), physical illness, and stressful events.  In California alone about 500,000 adolescents have faced depression. Mental Health America’s studies show during 2022 there was an increase of 197,000 of reported Major Depressive Episodes. Starting and continuing throughout Covid-19, children of all ages have faced mental health struggles.

Preventions

        

Depression may seem like a never ending cycle of feeling hopeless and alone but there are ways to help prevent depression. Building strong relationships, managing your stress, and more could change the way you feel, think, and act. Having strong relationships like friends, teachers, or family members could bring you the support you need to keep going. When not feeling like yourself and need someone to talk to, calling or meeting up with someone that you trust can go a long way. According to the University of Cambridge, researchers found that “supportive family or friends in early adolescence could help reduce depressive symptoms in later teenage years” and, “supportive friends and family environments may help enhance children’s ability to cope with adverse situations by improving their self-esteem and offering stress-relief and through helping them develop effective interpersonal skills.” When times get hard, sometimes we need support and comfort from the people around us.

Stress is one of the main reasons why teens are depressed which is why it is important to manage it. The WHO (World Health Organization) states “stress can be defined as a state of worry or mental tension caused by a difficult situation.” Whereas “depression is a mood disorder that causes a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest”, according to the Mayo Clinic. For teens, stress can mostly come from school. Apa.org (American Psychological Association) explains that “Young children might be stressed about making friends, dealing with bullies, or getting along with their teachers. They might also be anxious about tests and grades.” Worrying over tests, grades, bullies, and more are frequent things that students/teens stress over about at school. Taking breaks and taking care of yourself are ways to help relieve stress. Doing excessive work at one time could overwhelm a person quickly and stress would start to build up. Without taking a break, you’re not giving yourself a rest to come back and resume with new energy. Thewellbeing.org states, “Taking breaks has been shown to be important in recovering from stress, which can, in turn, improve your performance. Recovering from work stress can restore energy and mental resources and decrease the development of fatigue, sleep disorders and cardiovascular disease.”

Self Care

Not only is it good to take breaks but taking care of yourself could help you prevent depression. Doing things like eating well could improve yourself and help you get out of your depressive state. When taking care of yourself, doing something positive for yourself is a robust coping strategy. CDC (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention) says that “Studies have shown higher rates of obesity in persons with depression.” Bad eating habits could lead to problems with your mental and physical health and that would not help you deal with depression. Brain chemicals are triggered by foods people eat. Eating foods lacking in carbohydrates cause a lack of chemicals that help promote feelings of happiness.Giving yourself the self-care you need is important especially when dealing with depression because your mental health determines how you deal with your stress. Doing the simplest tasks you enjoy doing can lighten your mood and increase your energy, that's why self-care is a great option to try and help with your depression.

There are many different ways you can take care of yourself, for instance finding a new hobby or even maybe a hobby you're already interested in can be a way to distract yourself and relieve your stress. Exercise is another good way to help your depression because it releases your brain endorphins which makes your brain feel happy. It's a good way to keep yourself active and your mind distracted. From the Mayo Clinic, “Research on depression, anxiety and exercise shows that the psychological and physical benefits of exercise can also help improve mood and reduce anxiety.” If you're more into relaxing activities then trying something like meditation or yoga is a good option for you.

                                        Conclusion

Depression is a dark place to be in and it’s okay to not be okay. It causes lack of motivation and less happiness in daily life. Many teens experience these issues and the number continues to rise. Take time to take care of yourself, with activities you enjoy. Ask for help when you need it because this is something that you can’t do alone. You’re not alone and there are people here for you.
