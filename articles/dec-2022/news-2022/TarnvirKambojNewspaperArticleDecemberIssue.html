                                        Introduction

Many people have either played with VR (Virtual Reality) or have heard of it. With it being more affordable than ever, more people are buying these headsets. VR headsets enable you to “teleport” into another universe (sometimes known as the metaverse) and be in someone else’s shoes. The headsets allow you to escape reality and be someone else for once. A new type of VR has now risen called Nerve Gear. The Oculus founder claims that this headset will kill you if you lose in the game.

                                        Who is this man?                                                 

           

Palmer Luckey was Oculus's founder and creator before selling it to Mark Zuckerburg for $2.3 billion in 2014. After selling the Oculus he turned his brain power to the Nerve Gear. The Nerve Gear will kill you in real life if you die in the game. Palmer Luckey says “The idea of tying your real life to your virtual has always fascinated me - you instantly raise the stakes to the maximum level and force people to fundamentally rethink how they interact with the virtual world and the players inside it.” He believes that this will change the Virtual Reality landscape forever. This new headset will be the first of its kind. Luckey also claims that he built weaponized technology like this, giving his jump to VR to founding Anduril Industries. Anduril Industries is a defense company that brings cutting-edge AI software that will solve complex national security challenges for America and its allies. Needless to say that Luckey is more than qualified to create the Nerve Gear.

Nerve Gear: How does it Work?   

                        

 The Nerve Gear is inspired by the new game called Sword Art Online. Sword Art Online is a multiplayer online role-playing game set in a fantasy world. It is a unique storyline based on the anime. The Nerve Gear will have the players fight their way through a 100-floor dungeon to escape a mad scientist’s virtual world and if they survive, they will live in real life.  

 Nerve Gear has three “explosive charge modules” tied to a “narrow band photo sensor that can detect when the screen flashes red at a specific frequency” as stated in Palmer Luckey’s blog. When the screen flashes red at a specific frequency the charge modules fire obliterating the person's head which leaves the person dead. The three horns that are on the top of the VR are the explosive charge modules. Even though he has created a way to kill the user, the perfect VR equation is still far. Palmer Luckey has toyed around with the idea of creating a VR headset that can not be removed or tampered with and forces that player to stay in the game or he will die.

 Why is Palmer Luckey making this?

Murderous video games had always fascinated Luckey as he tweeted a year before “The concept of video games with physical consequences as severe as death are a sci-fi staple but seen as beyond pale in real life. Given the popularity of motorsports, extreme athletics etc: Why?”  He believes that if we humans can put our life on the line every day just for enjoyment, why can’t we play video games that put our life on the line? As I was reading this on Palmer Luckey's Twitter, I looked at the comment section and people were saying that we don’t put our life on the line because why would we?  We could spend our time with family and friends and live life to the fullest, not spending our time playing through a 100-floor dungeon that has a mad scientist trying to kill us. I even asked a few people about their opinion on Nerve Gear. They said the idea is riveting, but most people will not buy it.  

                                Is it even Legal?

 Hypothetically, the Nerve Gear could potentially be legal but it would take a variety of factors. First Palmer Luckey has to go to court and patent the invention so nobody can take the credit Wipo.int says “patent protection means that the invention cannot be commercially made, used, distributed, imported or sold by others without the patent owner's consent.” According to Asme.org the Nerve Gear has to be patented in different countries so other people can’t steal it and cannot create their own type of the Nerve Gear. By patenting this Luckey can sell this in the country.

Conclusion

VR is a thing that people enjoy daily and many people have heard of it. Palmer Luckey, who created this invention is ready to perfect this in the coming future. This invention may start a new era of technology. Entrepreneurs and businessmen alike are fascinated and excited about what this may hold for our future.
