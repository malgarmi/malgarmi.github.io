Red Giants: How long does the sun have to live, before it explodes?

The sun gives us heat, light and sometimes skin cancer.  We depend on the sun to give us heat, and light to sustain life for the next 5 billion years. But  the sun’s energy isn’t unlimited and one day the sun  will eventually collapse. The sun gets energy from a nuclear fusion. A nuclear fusion is when hydrogen atoms collide in the sun’s core to fuse and create helium atoms. This process is called  proton-proton, and it not only emits a large amount of energy, but it’s what keeps the sun stable. Scientists and astronomers claim  that the sun is composed of 75% hydrogen, and 25% helium, basically a hot ball of gas. Scientists also predict that the sun has lived half its life of about 4.5 billion years, and its radius has grown by  6%. But, what will happen to the sun after 5 Billion years? After 5 billion years, the sun will become what's called a red giant(a dying star). As stars age, they go through various phases that are so diverse that it may be hard to believe that they are the same star. Just like a plant seed growing into a small stem, eventually becoming a tree. Red giants are fairly rare in space, and  are created after they run out of their  fuel hydrogen  which is needed for nuclear fusion(the process that makes the sun and stars shine and have energy) and therefore, has begun the process of slowly  dying. A star keeps  its stability going through a balance between the gravity  which holds it together  and the pressure moving outwards from ongoing thermonuclear fusion processes(the way energy is made for it) happening at its core. This causes the star to expand. The stars usually go from H+HE (Hydrogen and Helium); to HE+N (Helium and Nitrogen); to H+C (Helium and Carbon); to O+C (Oxygen and Carbon); to O+Ne+Mg (Oxygen, Neon, and Magnesium); to Si+S (Silicon and Sulfur); and finally to a hard, solid Ni+Fe (Nickel and Iron) core. But what happens to these red giants when they, too, die?

The Speed of Light, Planetary Nebula’s & White Dwarfs: The End?

When these red giants die, they collapse and become white dwarfs. Once this happens, a  Planetary Nebula  is what remains of the Red Giant. This is a glowing shell of ionized gas which is discharged from Red Giants. What's interesting about these, is the dwarf star in the center of the nebula, a beautiful, small, but dense white dwarf. They contain a low luminosity, an insane amount of mass and density, and a radius the size of Earth. The central region of a typical white dwarf consists of a mixture of Oxygen and Carbon which is surrounded by a thin envelope of helium, and an even thinner layer of hydrogen. It is usually 100,000+ Kelvin  as there is no fusion reaction occurring(no warm energy) unless it's accreting matter from a nearby star, it cools, and eventually becomes invisible in the span of a billion years. One example for a white dwarf,and planetary nebula is called NGC2440, NGC2440 is located in the constellation Puppis. Its host star(the star where planets or lesser objects revolve around), HD 62166 is potentially the hottest white dwarf star discovered, with a temperature of  10^7, .6 solar mass, and 0.028 solar radius. The nebula was first discovered on March 4 1790, by William Herschel, who described it as; "a beautiful planetary nebula of a considerable degree of brightness, not very well defined.". It is located  roughly 4000 light years away. That means even if you were traveling at the speed of light, it would take you 4000 years! But, how would we even get to the speed of light?
