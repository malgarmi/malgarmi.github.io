Introduction

Do parallel universes exist?

Studies have hinted at possible detections of parallel universes. Physicists believe there are various ways of creating alternative or parallel universes. Parallel universes are no longer just a feature of a good sci-fi story. There are now some scientific theories that support the idea of parallel universes beyond our own.Eventually, matter has to repeat itself and arrange itself in similar ways. So if the universe is infinitely large, it is also home to infinite parallel universes.

Where did the idea of alternate dimensions come from?

Alternate Dimensions can  be any sort of phase shifting where you go through worlds, any sort of vision that lets you see things normal people can't or worlds that exist on top of our own. These are often confused with parallel universes when in actuality they are very different. Dimensions are constantly changing and unexpected.The idea of dimensions is not widely known but the earliest references to the dimensions in space date back to Aristotle during the 382 to 322 BC who stated it in one of his books about heaven. A dimension arises when a force is generated that allows movement into a domain. Although nothing has been directly observed that suggest that these forms actually exist, so far the evidence supporting the idea is purely theoretical.

Is there an end to the universe?

The Universe has been expanding for the last 13.8 billion years. So, the regions of space that are observable are much farther away. As of now the observable universe is a sphere with a volume of 410 nonillion.However, there is no reason to suspect this limit is an actual edge to the Universe or that what lies beyond this, if it exists, has an edge at all.Even if the Universe is finite, it does not have to have an edge in the same way that the earth does not have an edge.

What might be some differences between Universes?  

        Let’s say that all this stuff about universes, multiverses, and whatnot is true. Will there be any differences between universes? According to the (found below) “...in any finite region of space matter can only arrange itself in infinitely many different configurations. For example, if I get a deck of cards, and shuffle the cards I will get different orders, but after a possible infinite amount of times it has to repeat that order…” This means there can be different multiverses but not infinite. It also explains about universes similar to ours,  referred to as a parallel universe by cosmologists. Speaking of cosmology, a famous cosmologist known as Stephen Hawking, has explained this idea thoroughly in an interview. He says, “We are not down to a single, unique universe, but our findings imply a significant reduction of the multiverse, to a much smaller range of possible universes,” said Hawking. So there may not be infinite multiverses if there are any.

What is the inflation theory?

The inflation theory was developed around the 1980’s and was proposed by Alan Guth ,Andrei Linde, Paul Steinhardt, and Andy Albrecht. When you draw multiple dots on an un-inflated balloon and then begin inflating the balloon, the dots distance apart. Similarly, when the Big Bang it begins expanding but within a fraction of a second it stops. It expands so much that it creates the observable universe we know today.

        Another idea which adds onto the inflation theory is called “inflationary multiverse”. As stated in this article, “ it continues—just not in our universe where we could see it. And as it does, it spawns other universes. And even when it stops in those spaces, it continues in still others. This “eternal inflation” would have created an infinite number of other universes.” This means that inflation continues except not in our universe and it keeps on repeating creating multiverses.
