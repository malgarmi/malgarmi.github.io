Introduction

Polio, or poliomyelitis, is a terrible disease that affected many people and terrified the rest. It affects the nervous system and people are very likely to die because of it. Those who survived were hospitalized and left paralyzed. One of the most famous people to have been affected by polio was President Franklin Delano Roosevelt, better known as FDR, who contracted the potentially fatal virus in 1921. For almost the whole rest of his life, FDR would either walk with steel braces or sit in a wheelchair.

When the vaccine emerged, every person took it. This prevented polio from spreading so well that the last case happened in the 1980s. In 2018, there were only three countries with live polio. However, in New York the appearance of polio in an unvaccinated adult and contaminated sewer water has caused the New York governor, Kathy Hochul, to declare “... a State disaster emergency for the entire State of New York through October 9, 2022…”, according to Executive Order 21.

The Recent Occurrence

Polio has now once again reappeared in sewage and an unvaccinated adult. The individual tested positive for a poliovirus that had been in circulation since the 1990s.  According to New York state Health Commissioner Mary Bassett, “Based on earlier polio outbreaks, New Yorkers should know that for every one case of paralytic polio observed, there may be hundreds of other people infected … the department is treating the single case of polio as just the tip of the iceberg of much greater potential spread.” Polio could very well start another menacing threat to our country as it did in the 1940s. This recent detection of polio has caused many scientists to urge New Yorkers to get vaccinated. Since many don’t know what polio is, less than 70% of kids in some neighborhoods are vaccinated. This puts infants at a much higher risk, which could cause this virus to jump from host to host rapidly. In addition, the absence of a direct cure shows the importance of the vaccine.

This disease’s most severe symptom is paralysis, something that can lead to permanent disability and death. Even children who may seem like they recovered from polio, can develop muscle pain, weakness or paralysis as adults 15-40 years later. The public needs to spread the word of vaccines so that we can protect the world’s children and stop this dangerous virus from killing again. Polio was so big of an issue in the 1950s that about 300,000 to 400, 000  people got infected each year. This clearly shows why an emergency was widely declared.

The History of Polio

                Several polio epidemics occurred from 1948 to 1955, before a vaccine was available. People were so scared of the virus that they avoided any crowded, public places and regularly checked their children for symptoms. More than 2 thousand people had died and everyone was terrified. According to History.com, a 1952 survey found that Americans feared only nuclear annihilation more than polio. This shows that polio was a major issue on many people's minds. Thousands of people were worried that their children or they could contract polio to the point of spraying their properties with pesticide!

 

Parents forbade their children from playing with new friends – much like we did when the COVID-19 pandemic began. People whose lungs were paralyzed were placed in machines known as iron lungs, which helped them breathe when their own lungs had failed them.

Fortunately, Dr. Jonas E. Salk and his colleagues had been working on developing a polio vaccine since 1952. Three years later, the vaccine was licensed in the U.S., and a second vaccine, developed by Dr. Albert B. Sabin, was licensed in 1961. A third vaccine was licensed in 1963.

How Dangerous Is Polio?

        Polio is a very threatening and contagious illness that is currently in New York State. It was recently found in an individual and the New York State governor, Kathy Hochul,  has declared an emergency.  In its most severe form, it can cause nerve injury (or paralysis), difficulty breathing and even death. The symptomatology of paralysis consists of weakness in the muscles, so that the person finds difficulty in walking, and when it progresses to muscle wasting, the person is totally bedridden or crippled. Polio could be very dangerous. If it is not controlled in time, it could be a serious problem, and more epidemics could happen.

Polio is transmitted to humans through ingestion. It travels through the blood supply to the nerves, This is how it enters the nervous system, and infects the cells that help the muscles work. The virus of poliomyelitis is ingested by infected water, infected food, or a contaminated needle on injections. Poliovirus affects the muscle system by wasting the muscles, which is a major source of disability.

The Efforts to End Polio

The vaccines and initiatives helped control new cases and prevent spread. In later years, organizations across the world helped make the vaccines available to the vast majority of the population across the world spreading awareness of polio as we are doing now. One especially important organization is the Rotary Organization, who helped found the Global Polio Initiative. Other initiatives have happened since the 1938 March of Dimes, which started the National Foundation for Infantile Paralysis.  All of these efforts had apparently ended polio. Even the WHO had said, “... 99 percent of cases have fallen.” Even though it was true in the 1950s, polio has appeared again.

The vaccines and initiatives are helping control new cases and prevent spread. Organizations across the world are making vaccines available to the vast majority of the population across the world spreading awareness of polio. People are getting vaccinated by doctors around the world. These campaigns are making huge impacts for countries such as Afghanistan where much of the infantine population is battling polio.

Conclusion

Polio is a fatal disease that stretches its history back to the ancient time, however, it has had its rises and falls since the 1900’s. Vaccines and better health treatments for those paralyzed have prevented the spread of polio and eventually many countries have eliminated polio. However in recent years, people have been taking this infection less seriously, causing many countries to get affected by polio again such as the United States. To prevent it, take the vaccine!

Stay healthy, stay safe and stay away from New York!!!
