¿California Ghost Stories?

By: “Desk Champions”- Kavya, Jasmine, and Isaiah

Ghost Stories? Pffft, lame. Those don’t scare us. But these stories will leave you stunned and scarred for life. There have been many places with strange, paranormal, and supernatural activity spotted all around California. We have picked 3 stories that will send chills down your spine and make you wish you never saw the day again. So get ready and buckle up as we explore these unexplained phenomena.  

The Haunted Room of Hotel del Coronado

In 1892, on Thanksgiving day, Kate Morgan checked into Hotel del Coronado in Coronado, California. She seemed to be waiting for someone, and after five days of waiting, she took her own life. According to the hotel’s guests, she was alone and unhappy. She’d bought a handgun—perhaps for hunting, you may have thought—but nope. The city’s coroner later confirmed that Kate had shot herself and her dead body lay on the staircase of the hotel. It was a tragic incident and since then, many believe Kate’s ghost haunts room 3327, the room in which she had once stayed.

        Many people believe her spirit has not left that room yet because of the many ghostly events that have occurred since then. The flickering lights, the breezes that come from nowhere, all the way to the unexplained footsteps and voices. Some claim to have seen Kate Morrgian strolling in the hotel hallways and the seashore. Paranormal researchers have detected suspicious activity in room 3327; this may be why everyone always books room 3327 when they come to Hotel del Coronado. A lot of people have claimed to see all kinds of paranormal activity, but who knows? You’ll never really know unless you go to Hotel Del Coronado located at 1500 Orange Avenue.

The Queen Mary Haunted Boat Hotel

The boat had its opening on September 26, 1934, by none other than Queen Mary herself. It only sailed for a short period of time until they decided to stop running and the ship retired. Subsequently, they have turned it into a hotel on Long Beach California where people can now book and stay. But who knows what might be lurking in the shadows.  There have been several cases of employees witnessing people disappearing into thin air and ghosts spotted roaming around the boat. Some even say a few of the rooms are haunted… There are 3 rooms that got the most complaints and ghost sightings, The Mayfair Room, Room B240, and the Poolroom. The first area is B340. This is the room that has the most supernatural activity. According to travelandleasure.com, Walter J. Adamsonin, a 3rd class person who booked this room, died in 1948 in this specific room. No one to this day knows the cause of his death. Also, employees would report that the water would randomly turn on and the lights would start to flicker. All of that happened in this room. One woman saw a man disappearing into thin air in front of her bed! Then there is also the first-class swimming pool, which is the most popular place to have paranormal activity. This may not be real, but some people have spotted a woman wearing a wedding gown sitting next to a young boy in a suit. Then there is a middle-aged woman in a tennis outfit with a racket. Then finally, there is a little girl that only appears for a few seconds and then disappears. The girl also appears in the boiler with an old ragged teddy bear. Another matter was in the Mayfair Room, where a woman was working and cleaning. She felt a slight brush up on her chair while sitting down to rest. She also says that she felt unusually cold until she saw a transparent figure walking across the room and roaming around. She rushed out of the room and reported the figure.

The Niles Canyon Ghost/ The White Witch

Every year on February 26th, a woman in white can be seen near Niles Canyon and around the Sunol area. She’s been called many different names over the years, most notably the “White Witch '' as well as the “Niles Canyon Ghost'', and the story around her has been retold many times, with the fear invoked to whoever hears about the tale remaining the same every time.

One story is the “Vanishing Hitchhiker” type. Since February 26th, 1938 (the first time this specific story was told), a woman in a white gown can be seen near the side of Niles Canyon road asking to be driven to a San Francisco address, many picking her up. However, everyone that has taken her in says that she disappeared out of their car once they reached the Dumbarton Toll Plaza, never to be seen by them again.

Another story goes that a woman was killed when she was coming from her wedding at the intersection next to where Highway 84 meets Highway. Victoria Christian, a Fremont resident, lists in her memoir “Around Sunol” that this story along with many others have been retold for generations. The woman was riding in the night, when her carriage’s horses were all of a sudden shocked, launching the carriage with her in it. This ultimately killed her. Now, she can be seen at her death site wearing the same wedding gown she died in.

Conclusion
        The three locations written about here are just a fraction of the many areas that have experienced supernatural, haunted activities. Each individual story has its own history and backstory behind them, and whether or not these stories are backed up by scientific evidence or are just hoaxes, they all invoke the same emotions: fear, exhilaration, and curiosity.
