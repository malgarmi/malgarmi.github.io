Newspaper Article September Issue:

A typical newspaper article contains five (5) parts:

Headline: This is a short, attention-getting statement about the event.

Byline: This tells who wrote the story.

Lead paragraph: This has ALL of the who, what, when, where, why, and how in it. A writer must find the answers to these questions and write them into the opening sentence(s) of the article.

Explanation Section: After the lead paragraph has been written, the writer must decide what other facts or details the reader might want to know. The writer must make sure that he/she has enough information to answer any important questions a reader might have after reading the headline and the lead paragraph. This section can also include direct quotes from witnesses or bystanders. Also, the explanation section should consist of multiple paragraphs.

Concluding Information: This section ties up any loose ends. The writer must summarize the main points in the article within this section and attempt to end on a strong note.

Lastly: Please try to incorporate all three rhetorical appeals into your article. Here are some questions to consider when using rhetorical appeals: Ethos- What does your article do to build credibility/trust with your audience? Pathos- Which emotions is your article using to appeal to readers? Logos- What claims are your article making and how are you supporting your claims?

The Haunted Room of Hotel del Coronado

        In 1892, on Thanksgiving day, Kate Morgan checked into Hotel del Coronado[f] in Coronado, California. She seemed to be waiting for someone, and after five days of waiting, she took her own life. According to the hotel’s guests, she was alone and unhappy. She’d bought a handgun—perhaps for hunting, you may have thought—but nope. The city’s coroner later confirmed that Kate had shot herself and her dead body lay on the staircase of the hotel. It was a tragic incident and since then, many believe Kate’s ghost haunts room 3327, the room in which she had once stayed.[g][h][i][j][k][l]

        Many people believe her spirit has not left that room yet because of the many ghostly events that have occurred since then. The flickering lights, the breezes that come from nowhere, all the way to the unexplained footsteps and voices. Some claim to have seen Kate Morrgian strolling in the hotel hallways and the seashore. Paranormal researchers have detected suspicious activity in room 3327; this may be why everyone always books room 3327 when they come to Hotel del Coronado. A lot of people have claimed to see all kinds of paranormal activity, but who knows? You’ll never really know unless[m] you go to Hotel Del Coronado located at 1500 Orange Avenue.

The Queen Mary Haunted Boat Hotel

The boat had its opening on September 26, 1934, by none other than Queen Mary herself. It only sailed for a short period of time until they decided to stop running and the ship retired. Subsequently,[n][o][p] they have turned it into a hotel on Long Beach California where people can now book and stay. But who knows what might be lurking in the shadows.  There have been several cases of employees witnessing people disappearing into thin air and ghosts spotted roaming around the boat. Some even say a few of the rooms are haunted… There are 3 rooms that got the most complaints and [q][r][s][t][u]ghost sightings[v][w][x][y][z], The Mayfair Room, Room B240, and the Poolroom.

One woman saw a man disappearing into thin air in front of her bed! Then there is also the first-class swimming pool, which is the most popular place to have paranormal activity. This may not be real, but some people have spotted a woman wearing a wedding gown sitting next to a young boy in a suit. Then there is a middle-aged woman in a tennis outfit with a racket. The Niles Canyon Ghost/ The White Witch

Every year on February 26th, a woman in white can be seen near Niles Canyon and around the Sunol area. She’s been called many different names over the years, most notably the “White Witch '' as well as the “Niles Canyon Ghost'', and the story around her has been retold many times, with the fear invoked to whoever hears about the tale remaining the same every time.

        One story is the “Vanishing Hitchhiker” type. Since February 26th, 1938 (the first time this specific story was told), a woman in a white gown can be seen near the side of Niles Canyon road asking to be driven to a San Francisco address, many picking her up. However, everyone that has taken her in says that she disappeared out of their car once they reached the Dumbarton Toll Plaza, never to be seen by them again.

Another story goes that a woman was killed when she was coming from her wedding at the intersection next to where Highway 84 meets Highway. Victoria Christian, a Fremont resident, lists in her memoir’ “Around Sunol” this story along with many others have been retold for generations. The woman was riding in the night, when her carriage’s horses were all of a sudden shocked, launching the carriage with her in it. This ultimately killed her. Now, she can be seen at her death site wearing the same wedding gown she died in.

Conclusion
        The three locations written about here are just a fraction of the many areas that have experienced supernatural, haunted activities. Each individual story has its own history and backstory behind them, and whether or not these stories are backed up by scientific evidence or are just hoaxes, they all invoke the same emotions: fear, exhilaration, and curiosity.
