Top 10 Manga and Books For Middle School Readers

By: Director/CEO Shivam Srivastava, Illustrator/ Tarnvir Kamboj, Editor/Credits Rayaan Haque, Contributor Vaishnav Kumar Bette.

Reading could be your thing to do during your free time but good books can be challenging to find. In this article we suggest some of the top books for middle schoolers, the books we suggest include topics such as crime, adventures, humor and fantasy.

#10: Hunter X Hunter (Manga)

Gon Freecss is a teenage boy living on Whale Island. Gon goes through an adventure to obtain his Hunter License, and become a godly hunter. He learns from his father's disciple Kite  that his father, who was taught to be   dead, is still alive somewhere as a top Hunter, risking his life to seek unknown items, such as hidden treasures, unreal moments, exotic living creatures, etc. If you're looking for a manga that's not too short, not that long, and catchy, this is a must.

#9: Demon Slayer (Manga)

Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba (鬼滅の刃, Kimetsu no Yaiba, "Blade of Demon

Destruction") is a Japanese manga series illustrated by Koyoharu Gotouge. It starts with a young teenage boy, named Tanjiro Kamado, who ought to become a Demon Slayer after his family was annihilated and his younger sister, Nezuko Kamado, was turned into a demon, by an interesting and pure evil antagonist, named Muzan Kibutsuji. He sets off on a quest to take revenge on the Demon King (Muzan) and learns the way of hinokami.

#8 Spy School 

The book Spy School is about a 12-year-old kid named Ben Ripley. The book is intended to be a fictional book about Ben's life and some people who could potentially be acting evil as part of a big mystery. The action/mystery-packed story starts with an anonymous person in the protagonist's house who says they are a member of the CIA. After that series of questioning events, Ben gets recruited to a secret school for spies because of his abnormally good math skills. If you're a bookworm and are looking for an engaging book to read, this is definitely for you.

Rating (Global) 3.7 Stars

#7 Slacker

The cartoony-based book, named Slacker, is a book by Gordan Korman that features Cameron Boxer as a Slacker. He spends his life avoiding school work, teachers and homework. His mom told him to turn off the oven however he forgot to do so, resulting in his house almost burning down. His parents were tired of Cameron playing video games, so they gave him an option to join a club or create one. Cameron and his buddies established a fake club resulting in him having the opportunity to play video games and keep his parents happy. Although this club was not real, a kid named Daphne Leibotiz brought this club to the attention of Mr. Fanshaw and contacted Cameron Boxer. Will Cameron Boxer be able to keep his slacker lifestyle and keep his parents happy, or will Cameron’s plan fail horribly?

#6: Percy Jackson and the Olympians

Percy Jackson is a character that has dyslexia and learns that he obtained powers to control water specifically given by his father. Always trouble-prone, the life of teenager Percy Jackson  gets a lot more complicated when he learns he's the son of the Greek god Poseidon. Since he was a demigod, many monsters tried killing him. At a training ground for the children of deities, Percy practices and trains to harness his divine powers and prepare for an adventure. Soon he was chosen for a prophecy in which he had to save the world. In the adventure, Percy prevented a feud among the Olympians from erupting into a devastating war on Earth and rescued his mother from the clutches of Hades, god of the underworld.

#5 Attack on Titan (Manga)

The best-selling manga, “Attack on Titan” is set in a world where humanity is forced to live in cities and towns (only to eventually be covered with tremendous amounts of blood) surrounded by three enormous walls that protect them from gigantic man-eating humanoids referred to as Titans; the story follows the protagonist Eren Yeager, who vowed, promised to exterminate/slaughter all Titans after they bring about the destruction of his hometown and the death of his mom. He eventually becomes a fighter and *SPOILER ALERT* a huge plot twist, and conflict happens in the story, near the end.

#4: The Hunger Games

The Hunger Games is a story about a girl named Katniss Everdeen as she lives in a dystopian world where people are chosen to fight each other for entertainment . When the younger sister of Katniss primrose  is chosen for this event Katniss herself  volunteers in Primrose's place and the only thing Katniss has to do is survive.The book has won 15 accolades and The Guardians top 10 best books of the 21st century. I would highly recommend this book as the dialogue and story were both amazing and definitely deserve those awards.

#3 City of Ember

City Of Ember takes place in a city meant to house survivors with enough supplies to last them 200 years. However after this period of time the supplies left begin to run extremely low.There also is a locked box passed down from the mayor of the city to the next  mayor of the city but the last mayor died of a cough before he could pass down the box.   \Despite this life is going on normally within the city as the 2 characters Lisa and Doon graduate and get jobs. This changes when Lisa's very young sister discovers a hiding place with the locked box and the instructions however eats them since she is a toddler. The city of ember was really entertaining to read as the world building, story, and characters really got me invested. I would highly recommend it.

#2 The Giver

       

The Giver is a young book about a boy named Jonas who lives in a futuristic society that has no pain,fear,war and hatred. Jonas is chosen to be the receiver who learns about the past conflicts and history of this society. This society tries to be a utopia but the reader will see this as a dystopia. The Giver has an amazing story to tell and it is hard to put down. The Giver won the 1994 John Newbarry medal, says ala.org and has sold more than 12 million books worldwide. #1 One Piece (Manga)

Monkey .D. Luffy is on his quest to become king of  pirates, and goes on a unlasting adventure, through lots of challenges, hard weather, betrayal, and violence to achieve his dream.

With over 1058 chapters, and is STILL ongoing, arguably the best manga ever created, One Piece is a masterpiece. It may seem long, however but once you get to the story and you completely understand the storyline you´ll beg for more. Hypebeast.com says One Piece has sold over half a billion copies and shattered a previous Guinness world record.If you're looking for an engaging story that won't end, for a while, this manga/anime is a MUST.
