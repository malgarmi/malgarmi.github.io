A field of sunflowers grows inside my brain

Beauty in form, yet all they bring is pain

Reaching high for a sun that isn’t there,

It’s beginning to be too much for me to bare

When Joy moved in, so did Pain

For loss accompanies every gain

There is no green grass side

Without a raging river a mile wide

Now invasive species float my mind

Inside my head they do bind

My greatest inventions to their chairs,

My spirit animal by its hairs

Just like a farmer to his land

And a musician with his hand

I myself grew this field of flowers,

I chose to nurture it for hours

 

I myself am to blame

Before the seeds ever came,

I let my mind become the soil

Rich in anger and turmoil

Without a sun they should have died,

For rules of nature they do abide

Now their barbed roots have dug deep,

Feeding on the thoughts that make me weep

These flowers that feast on my broken fears,

Drink from the streams of my blood-red tears,

Stems drag forgotten memories to the surface,

Leaves crowd the ones with joyful purpose

This is an illness I cannot escape

It is not one cured by a doctor in a cape

A mighty prison I have created for myself

With every nightmarish, book-filled shelf

One that resides inside my mind,

It is one of the invisible kind

The ones that take the slowest to kill,

Draining every last bit of my living will

I wish to seek help to demolish this weed,

To say from this sickness I have been freed

I want it to end more than anything,

Before it forever takes my everything.

anushka.

she’s the champagne and white wine

to the rich people she wishes she was

she’s as elegant and precious as the moon she gazes at every night

crescent or full, a sign of times to the people gazing

like the simplicity of Miyazaki’s petals

or the one-by-one brush scars of Mr. Gogh

her kindness sprouts and buds and blooms

(and maybe, just maybe will never get to falling)

she shows her love and compassion discreetly

but I never fail to notice it every single time

like how there is no moment of scratching her head

before flying my carrier pigeon back to me

when I express any emotion of any such minuscule size

there is an unspoken agreement between me and words

unbeknownst to her, she is the moon

she shines and gleams like broken glass

she finds comfort in the night,

the quietness of it all in perfect mirrors

despite her shyness, a nurturer who yearns

and dreams to lose the heartache

whom you’ll be able to share all the quiet moments

you cherish so much without unwanted ellipsis

everyone's heart deserves her, I proclaim

the kind smile she reluctantly etches onto her blush lips

the kind laugh that shakes hands with happiness

the kind voice that reminds me her favorite color is the one that encases her

the kind warmth that embraces my shoulders when the place she swallows her words land there

the kind cheek I will never forget pecking with the music of laughter

the kind everything, anything I pray to remember

but until you find her, you know the moon will keep you company
