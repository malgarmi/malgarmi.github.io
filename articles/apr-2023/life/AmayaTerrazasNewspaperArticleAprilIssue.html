How the Covid-19 Lockdown Affected Teens

By Amaya Terrazas

The year 2020 has changed the way we live now majorly, especially teens. One of the major problems the lockdown caused was that more and more teens started struggling with mental health. Switching from in person school to online school quickly without knowing when you will go back has a big part in this as well. The covid 19 lockdown took away a whole year's worth of school from students causing them to miss out on lots of social interactions which are important for growing teens and overall children to be a part of.

How has Covid-19 Affected Teens' Education?

The big switch from in person school to online was tough for many teens, not being able to go to school, not being able to see friends, not being able to get taught in an actual classroom, and not being able to get the help needed right away was definitely a big change. Getting assigned lots of assignments every single day caused lots of stress to teens, especially when most of them were always due at 11:59 pm that day. According to https://www.washingtonpost.com/ teens aged from 14-18 have had a negative impact on their academics caused by the covid-19 lockdown. The online education was sometimes not so organized which caused stress to some students, internet problems were also very annoying to deal with especially when you are trying to join a meeting. Since most lessons were on virtual meetings students basically had free range to do whatever they wanted, teachers had no control over students working at home so it made it easier for students to not pay attention or to cheat or to just not do the work. This affects students now because after the year of being online and not taking the work seriously, coming back to in person school was a challenge causing them to be behind and having trouble understanding what's going on in classes.

Another big challenge with online education was all the distractions. Being at home with siblings and family while trying to do school can be very overwhelming. Lots of teens already struggle with paying attention, so doing online school with your family all at home can make it even worse. Being at home can also make learning more difficult for others because it can be very hard to pay attention when you are at your own house with many other things you'd rather be doing instead of sitting down on a computer doing assignments.

How has Teens Mental Health Been Affected by the Covid-19 Lockdown?

        Covid 19 has caused teens to miss a whole important year of school, the one year that was missed has affected many teens and left them struggling with mental health problems. A main cause of this is because teens got social interaction taken away from them, not being able to go to school and socialize with people after being used to it your whole life can really change your social skills. During this time, https://www.healthline.com/ states that 1 in 3 girls and 1 in 5 teen boys started experiencing or getting worse anxiety. Due to the lack of interactions, missing out on social activities and the switch to online school, depression rates on teens went up, grades started lowering and even lack in motivation.

Conclusion

        The covid-19 lockdown was a frustrating time leaving so many of us with unanswered questions. Some teens may still be dealing with problems caused by the lockdown, but thankfully it's over now. Covid-19 is still a virus that is going around but it's much more calm now and teens are finally able to go and live their teenager lives without needing to be locked up. Teens are now also able to attend in person school which can help their education go back up.
