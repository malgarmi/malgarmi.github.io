                                Number 5: Track and Field

Track and Field is a sport that is most popularly played in highschool and it includes the skills running, jumping and throwing. The first and only category we will look at is running. Running involves many different distances; Sprints, which are, 100 meters, 200 meters and 400 meter runs, Middle-Distance, which is 800 meters to 1600 meter runs, and then finally Long-Distance which is 3,000 meters 5,000 meters, and a 10,000 meter run.

Number 4: Volleyball

Volleyball is a well played sport in highschool, in volleyball the teams are split into 2 teams (Team A and B). The said teams tend to have a minimum of 10 players per team and the positions in volleyball are Setter, Libero, Outside Hitter, Opposite Hitter, and the Middle Blocker. The Setter in volleyball is one of the most used players on teams offense, usually aiding the opposite or outside hitter. The Libero serves as a secondary defense specialist to aid in keeping the ball from touching the ground on their team’s side. The Outside Hitter’s job is to be a defense for balls that go over the net and as well as be a main passer and final hit in the games. The Opposite Hitter acts as a multi-purpose tool as they can hit from the front and back row but also look for quick attacks and can substitute for setters and set for them if unable. Lastly Middle Blockers act as the first defense against the other team’s offense and try to keep the ball from hitting the ground like most players, but most similarly to the libero.

        Number 3: Soccer

Soccer, also referred to as football in many parts of the world, is a globally popular sport played by two teams, each consisting of 11 players. The goal of soccer is to score as many goals as possible by kicking the ball into the opponent’s net while preventing the other team from scoring in their own net. There are several positions in soccer with each player having their own role and responsibilities. Here is a brief explanation of each position:

1. Goalkeeper - The goalkeeper is responsible for guarding the goalpost and keeping the ball from entering the net. They use their hands to block or catch incoming shots and play a crucial role in starting attacks from the back.

2. Defenders - The defenders are responsible for protecting their goal and preventing the opposing team from scoring. They work to break down opposition attacks and win back possession of the ball. Depending on the formation, defenders can be center-backs, full-backs, or wing-backs.

3. Midfielders - Midfielders typically are the most mobile players on the field and play a crucial role in both attack and defense. They work to control the midfield, create chances, and support the forwards.

4. Forwards - The forwards play closest to the opponent's goal and are responsible for scoring goals. They try to break down the opposition's defense and create opportunities to score. Depending on the formation, forwards can be center-forwards, wingers, or second-strikers.

        

Number 2: Basketball

                        

Basketball is a fast-paced team sport in which two teams with five players each compete to shoot a ball through their opponent's hoop, located ten feet above the ground on opposing ends of the court. The team with the most points at the end of the game, which lasts for four quarters of 12 minutes each, wins. Basketball has five players on each team; Point Guard, Shooting Guard, Small Forward, Power Forward and Center.In basketball, each team has five players, and each player has a specific position and role. Here is a brief explanation of each basketball position:

1. Point Guard - The point guard is often considered the team leader and the primary ball-handler. They are responsible for bringing the ball up the court, setting up plays, and organizing the team's offense. Point guards are generally quick, agile, and have excellent ball-handling and passing skills.

2. Shooting Guard - The shooting guard is primarily responsible for scoring points by shooting from the perimeter or driving to the basket. They are often considered the team's best shooter and can also play defense by guarding the opposing team's shooting guard or small forward.

3. Small Forward - The small forward is a versatile player who can play both offensively and defensively. They usually have a combination of height and speed, allowing them to play in multiple positions. They're responsible for scoring points, rebounding, and playing defense.

4. Power Forward - The power forward position is typically a physical player who plays close to the basket. They are responsible for rebounding, and blocking shots while also having a solid outside shooting range.

5. Center - The center is usually the tallest player on the team and plays close to the basket both offensively and defensively. They are responsible for dominating the low post, rebounding the ball on both ends of the court, and blocking shots.

        

Number 1: American FootBall

American football is a team sport played between two teams of 11 players each. The objective of the game is to score points by carrying or throwing an oval-shaped ball into the opposing team's end zone. The team with the most points at the end of four quarters wins the game. In American football, each position has a specific role and responsibility on the field.

The quarterback is the leader of the offense and is responsible for throwing or handing off the ball to other players. The running back carries the ball and runs with it, while the wide receivers and tight ends catch passes from the quarterback. The offensive line blocks and protects the quarterback and ball carriers from the defensive team. On defense, the defensive line attempts to stop the opposing team's running game and put pressure on the quarterback. The linebackers play a hybrid role, both defending against the run and covering receivers. The defensive backs are primarily responsible for defending against passes and preventing the opposing team from scoring. Special teams players handle kicking, punting, and returning kicks and punts.
